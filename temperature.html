Temperature vs. Latitude

It is a well known fact that the temperature closest to the poles are among the coldest temperatures on Earth, and conversely areas closer to the equator tend to be some of the hottest. Using our data, I find this fact to be mostly true. While I was unable to pull data across all latitudes, the data does still show a trend that agrees with the aforementioned statement about temperature. The maximum temperatures noted across a year's worth of data peaks highest closer to the equator, and decreases as latitude increases (as observations move away from the equator).
